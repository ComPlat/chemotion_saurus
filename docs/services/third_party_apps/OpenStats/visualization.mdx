The **Visualization Tab** in OpenStats allows users to explore and visualize data
through several plot types, including *Boxplots*, *Scatterplots*, and *Lineplots*.  
Subsequently, the most important features, are described in detail.

## Investigate the treatment effect

import FillSideBar from '@site/static/img/ThirdPartyApps/OpenStats/FillSideBar.png';
import FillPlot from '@site/static/img/ThirdPartyApps/OpenStats/FillPlot.png';

Plants are measured at different CO<sub>2</sub> concentration and at two treatments called
*chilled* and *non-chilled*.
To visualize the effect of the treatments the fill color of the Boxplots can be set based on
the treatment group. As can be seen below for each concentration one boxplot for *chilled* plants (light blue)
and one for the *non-chilled* plants (green) are shown.

## Investigate the treatment effect

Moreover, plants are measured at two different locations, Mississippi and Quebec.
To check whether the location has an effect on the CO<sub>2</sub> uptake the original
plot can be split into sub-plots. To do this the column containing the location information
(called *Type*) has to be chosen in a drop down menu.
